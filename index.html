Fruits

  1. Apples
  2. Mangoes
  3. Oranges

1. Apples

Apples are a type of fruit that are widely cultivated and consumed around the world. They are generally round or oblong in shape and have a red, yellow, or green skin, depending on the variety. Apples are a good source of fiber and vitamins, and they are generally low in calories. They can be eaten fresh, cooked, or processed into products like juice, sauce, and cider. Apples are also an important ingredient in many recipes, including pies, tarts, and crisps.

2. Mangoes

Mangoes are a type of fruit that are native to South and Southeast Asia and are now grown in many tropical and subtropical regions around the world. They are round or oval in shape and have a smooth, thin skin that can range in color from green to yellow, orange, red, or a combination of these colors. The flesh of the mango is juicy and sweet, and it can be eaten fresh or used in a variety of dishes, including salads, salsas, and smoothies. Mangoes are a good source of vitamins A and C, and they are also a good source of fiber.

3. Oranges

Oranges are a type of citrus fruit that are native to Asia and are now grown in many parts of the world, including the United States, Brazil, and Spain. Oranges are round or oblong in shape and have a bright orange skin that is covered in a thin layer of oil. The flesh of the orange is divided into segments and is typically juicy and sweet. Oranges are a good source of vitamin C, and they are also a good source of fiber and other nutrients. They can be eaten fresh, juiced, or used in a variety of dishes and desserts.
